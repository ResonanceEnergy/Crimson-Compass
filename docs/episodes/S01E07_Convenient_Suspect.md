# S01E07: The Convenient Suspect

## Episode Overview
**Title:** The Convenient Suspect
**Case:** Decoy culprit frame
**Case Token:** Sabotage prevented; decoy arrested
**Shadow Token:** Exit plan staged before the crime
**Theme:** False flags, misdirection, pattern recognition

## Adventure Game Structure

### Starting Location: Agency Interrogation Wing
**Description:** Secure interview facility with interrogation rooms, observation areas, and evidence processing stations.

**Interactive Objects:**
- **Interrogation Room** (ENGAGE): Question the suspect
- **Observation Window** (OBSERVE): Watch from adjacent room
- **Evidence Processing** (ENGAGE): Examine collected items
- **Security Station** (PROTOCOL): Access surveillance footage

**Initial Inventory:**
- Case File Documents
- Digital Forensics Kit
- Interview Recorder
- Evidence Analysis Tools

### Key Characters (Text-Based Dialogue)

#### Helix (Administrative Coordinator)
**Description:** Conducting interviews and managing witness interactions.
**Dialogue Tree:**
- Interview strategy: "Let me handle the questioning. Watch for inconsistencies."
- Suspect assessment: "This suspect is too perfect. Evidence fits too cleanly."
- Witness coordination: "The witness story has holes. Let's dig deeper."

#### Optimus (Intelligence Analyst)
**Description:** Analyzing evidence chains and statistical anomalies.
**Dialogue Tree:**
- Statistical analysis: "Evidence chain probability: 0.01%. Too perfect to be real."
- Pattern recognition: "Digital footprints show fabrication patterns."
- Timeline reconstruction: "Suspect's alibi has unexplained 15-minute gaps."

#### ZTech (Technical Specialist)
**Description:** Investigating digital footprints and electronic evidence.
**Dialogue Tree:**
- Digital forensics: "Ghost account created with professional tools."
- Evidence verification: "Fingerprints are too clean. Pressed intentionally."
- System analysis: "Access logs show staging before the crime occurred."

#### Decoy Suspect
**Description:** Confused individual who appears to be the culprit but is actually framed.
**Dialogue Tree:**
- Confusion: "I don't understand. I was just doing my job."
- Defense: "I've never accessed those systems. This is a mistake."
- Cooperation: "I'll help however I can. I want to clear my name."

## Scene 1: Interrogation Analysis

### Location: Interrogation Room
**Description:** Stark room with table, chairs, two-way mirror, and recording equipment.

**Interactive Elements:**
- **Suspect Chair** (ENGAGE): Conduct interview
- **Evidence Display** (OBSERVE): Present case materials
- **Recording Console** (ENGAGE): Review statements
- **Exit Door** (MOVE): Access observation area

**Puzzle: Alibi Timeline Construction**
- **Objective:** Build and verify the suspect's activity timeline
- **Mechanics:** Arrange evidence and witness statements chronologically
- **Solution:** Timeline shows impossible gaps and fabricated events
- **Clue:** Digital records don't match physical evidence timestamps

**Interview Mechanics:**
- **Trust Building:** Gain suspect cooperation through careful questioning
- **Evidence Presentation:** Show inconsistencies to break down story
- **Observation Analysis:** Watch for tells and body language cues

## Scene 2: Evidence Locker Investigation

### Location: Forensic Evidence Room
**Description:** Organized storage facility with examination tables, analysis equipment, and evidence shelves.

**Interactive Elements:**
- **Examination Table** (OBSERVE): Analyze physical evidence
- **Computer Terminal** (ENGAGE): Access digital forensics
- **Chemical Analysis Station** (ENGAGE): Test evidence authenticity
- **Security Cabinet** (PROTOCOL): Access restricted items

**Puzzle: Evidence Chain Deconstruction**
- **Objective:** Separate genuine evidence from planted items
- **Mechanics:** Test authenticity and trace origins of each piece
- **Solution:** Multiple evidence pieces trace to same staging source
- **Clue:** Chemical residue indicates recent handling and planting

**Forensic Analysis:**
- **Fingerprint Testing:** Reveal intentional placement patterns
- **Digital Trail Analysis:** Uncover ghost accounts and fabrication
- **Chain of Custody:** Identify breaks in evidence handling

## Scene 3: Frame Job Revelation

### Location: Suspect's Residence
**Description:** Ordinary apartment that reveals the true scope of the framing operation.

**Interactive Elements:**
- **Computer Desk** (ENGAGE): Examine personal computer
- **Filing Cabinet** (OBSERVE): Check personal records
- **Hidden Compartment** (KIT): Discover concealed items
- **Surveillance Equipment** (ENGAGE): Find monitoring devices

**Puzzle: Exit Plan Discovery**
- **Objective:** Uncover the pre-staged escape and cover-up plan
- **Mechanics:** Connect seemingly unrelated clues and preparations
- **Solution:** Exit strategy planned before crime was committed
- **Clue:** Travel documents and false identities prepared in advance

**Conspiracy Unraveling:**
- **Pattern Recognition:** Connect frame job to larger operation
- **Motive Discovery:** Understand why the decoy was chosen
- **Real Culprit Identification:** Gather evidence pointing to true perpetrator

## Gameplay Flow

1. **Interrogation:** Question the suspect and identify inconsistencies
2. **Evidence Review:** Examine collected materials in locker
3. **Timeline Construction:** Build suspect's activity chronology
4. **Forensic Analysis:** Test evidence authenticity

6. **Culprit Identification:** Uncover the true perpetrator

## Difficulty Options

- **Easy:** Obvious inconsistencies, helpful suspect cooperation
- **Normal:** Standard evidence complexity and interview challenges
- **Hard:** Subtle clues, uncooperative suspect, time pressure

- **Truth Seeker:** Identify all evidence inconsistencies
- **Forensic Master:** Successfully authenticate all evidence pieces
- **Interrogation Expert:** Break through suspect defenses
- **Frame Buster:** Completely unravel the false flag operation
