# S01E11: Safe House

## Episode Overview
**Title:** Safe House  
**Case:** Cleanup crew tries to erase evidence  
**Case Token:** Cleanup neutralized; evidence preserved  
**Shadow Token:** Safe house contains a hidden exit protocol  
**Theme:** Evidence preservation, cleanup operations

## Scene 1: Evidence Threat

**Background:** Designated safe house, evidence storage rooms, security systems. Urgent but controlled atmosphere.

**Narrative:** The cleanup crew has moved to a designated safe house to destroy evidence. They're working methodically through the files.

**Puzzle:** Evidence Prioritization  
- **Objective:** Identify and secure critical evidence  
- **Mechanics:** Evidence catalog analysis, threat assessment  
- **Solution:** High-priority items isolated first  
- **Clue:** Digital metadata shows access patterns

**Dialogue:**

**Cleanup Leader:** "This is standard procedure. Nothing personal."

**Helix:** "Destroying evidence? That's very personal in my book."

**Evidence Tech:** "We have protocols. You can't just barge in here."

## Scene 2: Preservation Operation

**Background:** Evidence processing center, secure containers, analysis stations. Race against time atmosphere.

**Narrative:** The crew has been contained, but they're demanding legal representation. Evidence recovery is critical.

**Puzzle:** Chain of Custody  
- **Objective:** Maintain evidence integrity during transfer  
- **Mechanics:** Documentation and verification protocols  
- **Solution:** All evidence properly logged and sealed  
- **Clue:** Hidden exit protocol in safe house systems

**Dialogue:**

**Legal Observer:** "I must insist on proper procedures. Any contamination and this is worthless."

**Optimus:** "Chain of custody verified. Integrity: 100%."

**ZTech:** "Their cleanup software? Amateur hour. I backed it up before they could erase it."
