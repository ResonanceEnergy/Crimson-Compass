# S01E01: Welcome Packet

## Episode Overview
**Title:** Welcome Packet
**Case:** Corporate contractor access ring siphons early prototype builds
**Case Token:** Ring leader arrested; prototype recovered
**Shadow Token:** Access logs show recurring 11-minute time offsets (compass anomaly hint)
**Theme:** Introduction to the agency, team dynamics, and Crimson Compass mechanics

## Character Ensemble (Futurama Uncharted Vibes)

### The Closer (Player) - "Gasket's Replacement"
Fresh-faced detective brought in to fill the shoes of the legendary Gasket after his mysterious "incident" that rocked the agency. You're handed his old console and the Crimson Compass - a sleek piece of 22nd-century quantum navigation tech that looks like it should be guiding pirates across the seven seas, complete with brass casing and glowing runes that would make any steampunk enthusiast jealous. The contrast between its ancient-sounding name and cutting-edge holographic displays creates hilarious moments as you fumble with features like "truth vector mapping" and "deception field detection" while trying to maintain professional composure. Your arc involves discovering that this isn't just a tool - it's haunted by Gasket's digital ghost, causing quirky malfunctions like projecting his old case files as holographic flashbacks or suddenly switching to "classic mode" with sepia-toned filters and jaunty sea shanty soundtracks.

### Optimus (Agent Y) - "The Bumbling Boomer Executive"
The agency's top dog, a precision organizational genius with investigational and analytical skills that could map the entire criminal underworld in his sleep. But here's the catch - he's a boomer who peaked in the early 21st century, completely outpaced by the technology he oversees. Picture this: Optimus trying to use a quantum communicator like it's a rotary phone, yelling "Hello? Hello? Can you hear me?" into a device that responds with holographic emojis and auto-translated sarcasm. He's constantly mixing up terms - calling AI assistants "those talking computers" and referring to neural implants as "fancy earpieces." Yet his strategic brilliance shines through in the boardroom, where he orchestrates globe-spanning operations with the precision of a chess grandmaster. His arc involves learning to adapt to technological advancement, from accidentally hacking into the agency's coffee machine network to discovering he has a natural talent for quantum encryption despite not understanding how it works.

### Helix (Agent X) - "Big Sister Administrative Glue"
The spunky, socially aware administrative powerhouse who keeps this dysfunctional family of spies from imploding. As the agency's admin librarian, archivist, mission planner, executer, coordinator, and dispatch, she's the one who remembers everyone's coffee orders, mediates arguments between Optimus's old-school rants and ZTech's techno-babble, and somehow gets the paperwork filed on time despite constant explosions and high-speed chases. With her cute but no-nonsense attitude, she holds the male-dominated team together with warmth and administrative warfare, turning bureaucratic nightmares into smooth operations. Imagine her coordinating a globe-trotting investigation while simultaneously booking hotel rooms, arranging fake passports, and reminding Optimus to take his blood pressure medication. Her arc shows increasing concern about the compass's psychological toll on the team, as she notices how its anomalies start affecting everyone's behavior - from Optimus's technology frustrations spiking to ZTech's inventions becoming even more absurd.

### ZTech (Agent Z) - "Nerd Soldier Tech Genius"
The ultimate tech wizard and engineering super agent, a nerd with the determination of a front-line soldier commanding unlimited budget and resources. ZTech runs ZBRANCH from a secret facility that's equal parts DARPA lab and Willy Wonka's chocolate factory, staffed by over 250 AI agents and 5000 robots that handle everything from weapons development to cafeteria management. His gadgets are absurdly over-engineered masterpieces - a laser pointer that also brews coffee, predicts weather, and doubles as a lie detector, or a seemingly normal pen that can hack any system, translate 47 languages, and even function as emergency dental floss. With his thick glasses, pocket protector, and endless enthusiasm for "maximum absurdity with maximum function," ZTech creates inventions that often cause more problems than they solve, but somehow always work in the end. His arc involves pushing technological boundaries to ridiculous limits, like inventing a cloaking device that makes users invisible but also turns them into irresistible magnets for cats.

### Gasket (Agent G) - "Broken Prodigy"
The agency's legendary former agent, taken out of service after a mysterious "incident" that still haunts the team like a bad dream. Gasket was the original master of the Crimson Compass, that quirky piece of future tech with its old-sounding name, and his intuitive understanding of its anomalies makes him an unofficial wildcard presence. He shows up intermittently for maximum impact, providing coded warnings and fragmented insights that feel like pieces of a broken puzzle. With his weathered intensity and exaggerated features, Gasket embodies the detective noir aesthetic - stern but haunted, like a character who stepped out of a classic film but got caught in a quantum anomaly. His arc reveals that the compass isn't just a tool - it's a piece of his psyche, storing fragments of his trauma and creating those persistent 5% anomalies that keep cases from feeling completely resolved.

### NPCs
- **Ring Leader:** Corporate executive, smug, entitled, mid-50s, expensive suit, thinks he's untouchable
- **Contractor Minions:** Tech workers, nervous, mid-20s, lab coats, caught between loyalty and fear
- **Security Guard:** By-the-book, suspicious, early 40s, uniform, takes his job way too seriously

## Scene 1: Stakeout Introduction

**Background:** Urban rooftop overlooking corporate headquarters at dusk. City lights twinkle, rain-slicked streets below. Construction crane in distance.

**Narrative:** You arrive at the stakeout location just as the target emerges from the building. The street is quiet, too quiet. Your informant mentioned unusual activity, but this seems... wrong. As you activate the Crimson Compass for the first time, it hums to life with a holographic display showing "truth vectors" - glowing lines that map potential deception patterns in the area.

**Compass Mini-Puzzle:** First Activation
- **Objective:** Calibrate the compass by aligning truth vectors with environmental cues
- **Mechanics:** Rotate the compass to match holographic lines with suspicious elements (shadowy figures, unusual heat signatures)
- **Solution:** Align with the target's movement pattern to reveal hidden security cameras
- **Reward:** Early warning of approaching guards

**Dialogue:**

**Helix:** "Welcome to the team, Closer. I'm Helix - I keep these cowboys from killing each other. Or themselves. And this ancient relic in your hands? That's the Crimson Compass. Don't let the old-timey name fool you - it's cutting-edge quantum navigation tech that reveals 'truth vectors' through all the lies. Just... try not to let it start playing sea shanties in the middle of a stakeout."

**Optimus:** "Target acquired. Movement pattern suggests evasion training. Probability of armed resistance: 23%. And Closer - that compass you're holding? It's not just a tool. It's... particular about its users. Don't let it start feeling too comfortable."

**ZTech:** "Ooh, check this thermal scan! The building's hotter than it should be. Prototype cooling system maybe? Hey, that compass of yours - I helped calibrate it once. Nearly fried my favorite coffee-brewing laser pointer in the process. But man, the readings it gives... they're something else."

**Player Choice Options:**

1. **X (Helix):** "Maintain distance and observe. Patience is key in surveillance. And Helix - coffee order?"
   - **Consequence:** You hold back, watching from the shadows. The target seems nervous, constantly checking surroundings. Helix smiles and whispers your coffee preference into her comms.
   - **Dialogue:** Helix: "Good call. Let them show their hand first. And yes, your usual black coffee is already en route."

2. **Y (Optimus):** "Get closer for a better look. Risk assessment shows acceptable danger. Optimus - any intel on this 'truth vector' tech?"
   - **Consequence:** You move in closer, using the crowd for cover. The target spots something suspicious and picks up pace. Optimus adjusts his data glasses with a confused frown.
   - **Dialogue:** Optimus: "Movement detected. Adjusting trajectory. Truth vectors? Ah, yes. The compass shows... possibilities. Probabilities. Patterns that shouldn't exist."

3. **Z (ZTech):** "Call in backup immediately. Better safe than sorry. ZTech - what gadgets do we have for this?"
   - **Consequence:** You radio for immediate support. The response team arrives quickly, but the target has already slipped away. ZTech pulls out what looks like a normal pen but starts deploying drone swarms.
   - **Dialogue:** ZTech: "Backup's here! Wait, where'd they go? Oh, and this pen? It hacks, translates, and even flosses teeth in emergencies!"

4. **G (Gasket):** "Trust your gut - this setup smells like a trap. Abort and regroup. Gasket... you know this compass better than anyone."
   - **Consequence:** You pull back immediately. Moments later, you see figures moving in the shadows - you made the right call. Gasket appears briefly, his eyes haunted.
   - **Dialogue:** Gasket: "Not like this. Never like this. The compass... it remembers. It always remembers."

## Scene 2: Confrontation

**Background:** Abandoned warehouse district. Dim streetlights, puddles reflecting neon signs. Chain-link fences, graffiti-covered walls.

**Narrative:** The target leads you to an abandoned warehouse district. Multiple vehicles are parked suspiciously, and you can hear muffled voices from inside one of the buildings. As you approach, the Crimson Compass begins to vibrate, its holographic display showing erratic truth vectors that seem to bend around the warehouse like they're avoiding something.

**Puzzle:** Vehicle Identification  
- **Objective:** Match license plates to contractor database  
- **Mechanics:** Examine 3 suspicious vehicles, cross-reference with agency database  
- **Solution:** One vehicle belongs to the target company, others are decoys  
- **Clue:** Partial plate visible, database shows full registration

**Environmental Interaction:** Warehouse Perimeter
- **Choice:** Approach quietly vs. create diversion
- **Consequence:** Quiet approach reveals guard patrol patterns; diversion attracts attention but creates opening

**Dialogue:**

**Ring Leader:** "You have no idea who you're dealing with. This is bigger than you think. That compass you're waving around? It's just a fancy toy!"

**Helix:** "Oh honey, we've dealt with bigger. And dumber. And trust me, that 'fancy toy' has seen things that would make your corporate board meetings look like tea parties. Now, about those access logs..."

**Optimus:** "Evidence suggests otherwise. Your access logs show 47 unauthorized entries with recurring 11-minute offsets. The universe doesn't create patterns like that by accident. Or does it?"

**ZTech:** "Their security system? Child's play. I could hack it with a potato. Or this pen! It also brews coffee and predicts the weather. Maximum absurdity, maximum function!"

**Gasket:** (appearing suddenly, voice haunted) "The offsets... they're not random. The compass sees them. It always sees them. But some truths... they cut deep."

**Ring Leader:** (nervously adjusting his tie) "You people are insane! That device is malfunctioning - probably some cheap knockoff from the black market!"

**Helix:** (rolling her eyes) "Cheap knockoff? Sweetie, this compass costs more than your entire executive bonus package. And it's showing me you're lying through your perfectly capped teeth."

**Optimus:** (adjusting his data glasses) "Probability of deception: 94.7%. The compass doesn't lie. Unlike some corporate executives I could mention."

**ZTech:** (excitedly waving his multi-tool pen) "Ooh, let me scan him! This pen also detects lies - and brews decaf for stressful interrogations!"
